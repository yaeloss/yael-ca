By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

TAMPA — Florida Gov. **Rick Scott** was dealt a blow Thursday morning when a federal judge declared unconstitutional mandatory drug screening for state employees.

The ruling, [handed down](http://www.aclufl.org/pdfs/2012-04-26-ACLU-AFSCMEvScott.pdf) by U.S. District Judge **Ursula Ungaro** in the Southern District of **Florida**, issues a permanent injunction on the drug tests, which were required by an [executive order](http://www.flgov.com/wp-content/uploads/orders/2011/11-58-testing.pdf) in March 2011.

“The executive order does not identify a concrete danger that must be addressed by suspicionless drug-testing of state employees,” Ungaro wrote in the ruling.
