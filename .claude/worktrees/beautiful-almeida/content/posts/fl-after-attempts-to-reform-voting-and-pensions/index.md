By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> TAMPA— In the state of **Wisconsin**, it took an $80-million recall election to validate Gov. **Scott Walker**‘s fiscal reforms on public-sector collective bargaining and pension requirements for state employees.
> 
> In Florida, where similar legislation has been passed and signed into law, the biggest impediment to Gov. **Rick Scott**‘s agenda has not been the threat of a recall election but rather myriad lawsuits and constitutional challenges.
> 
> In the course of Scott’s 17 months in office, the laws or executive orders that have been held up by a court order or pending legislation include:
> 
> - Requiring drug testing for welfare recipients, [halted](http://www.miamiherald.com/2011/10/24/2470519/florida-welfare-drug-testing-halted.html) by Middle District Judge Mary Scriven in October 2011;
> - Requiring drug testing for state employees, [struck down](http://statehousenewsonline.com/2012/04/26/court-strikes-down-drug-testing-for-fl-state-workers/) by Southern District Judge Ursula Ungaro in April;
> - Requiring public employees to contribute 3 percent to their pension, [ruled unconstitutional](http://www.wptv.com/dpp/news/state/circuit-judge-jackie-fulford-strikes-down-fla-pension-contribution-law) by Northern District Judge Jackie Fulford in March;
> - Restricting early voting hours and ability of third party groups to register voters,[struck down](http://watchdog.org/19008/judge-strikes-one-statute-of-voter-reform-law/) by Northern District Judge Robert Hinkle in May;
> - Privatization of prisons, [struck down](http://www.postonpolitics.com/2011/11/scott-administration-moves-forward-with-prison-privatization-despite-court-ruling/) by Northern District Judge Jackie Fulford in November 2011;
> - Rejection of federal funds for high-speed rail projects, [upheld](http://jacksonville.com/opinion/blog/403455/larry-hannan/2011-03-04/florida-supreme-court-throws-out-high-speed-rail-lawsuit) by the State Supreme Court in March 2011;
> - Redrawing electoral districts, [rejected](http://www.google.com/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=8&ved=0CFkQFjAH&url=http://miami.cbslocal.com/2012/03/22/redistricting-map-approved-by-florida-senate/&ei=r6jXT6O9MIWK8QTJlcG5Aw&usg=AFQjCNFqg52RwYADF3JBtuBP2ofPhJ9wNw&sig2=MV_y9WI0tVkrj2yhil2Www) by state Supreme Court in March;
> - Prohibiting doctors from asking patients about firearms, [struck down](http://www.tampabay.com/news/courts/article1191547.ece) by Southern District Judge Marcia Cooke in September 2011;
> - Removing ineligible voters from county voter registration lists, [halted](http://www.scribd.com/doc/95500652/TPM-Docs-DOJ-Demands-Florida-Stop-Voter-Purge) by Justice Department order in May.
> 
> These are all issues that have served as the core focus for Scott and the Republican majority in the Legislature, elected during the tea party tidal wave of 2010, advocating fiscal responsibility and reduced public spending.
