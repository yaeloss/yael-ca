By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> TAMPA — If this past week in the Tampa Bay area is any indication, attendees of the [**2012 Republican National Convention**](http://www.google.de/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=rnc%202012&source=web&cd=3&ved=0CF0QFjAC&url=http://www.gopconvention2012.com/&ei=HELrT-L7CuOF4gT72Zj1Ag&usg=AFQjCNHRRH-f0at-Pu8iFTfF7E4jMy1EKg) should prepare themselves for a week of proposed reforms, party platforms — and tropical storms.
> 
> He said it’s impossible to predict if the ‘Big One’ will be headed Tampa’s way during the **RNC** in late August, but convention-goers should remain cautious in the face of storm season.“There is no way to forecast the presence of a tropical cyclone in the Gulf, or any other part of the Atlantic basin, this far in advance,” said **Dennis Feltgen**, communications officer for the [**National Hurricane Center**](http://www.google.de/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=national%20hurricane%20center&source=web&cd=1&ved=0CFQQFjAA&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.nhc.noaa.gov%2F&ei=xG3rT-_iKsrdtAa7tMHbBQ&usg=AFQjCNEbrVM9XSSJIRKz57u9BBPvLIDR3g) in **Miami**.
> 
> “It all depends upon the weather systems present at the time, as a tropical cyclone is steered by the weather pattern that surrounds it,” he told **Florida Watchdog**.
