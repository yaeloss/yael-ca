By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> TAMPA— It seems that after 17 months in office, Gov. [**Rick Scott**](http://www.google.de/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=rick%20scott%20%20&source=web&cd=10&ved=0CH4QFjAJ&url=http://www.flgov.com/&ei=jQfqT5TyF8Kh4gTe2IA5&usg=AFQjCNHtbgUGu3SflgaAlHypGq12clP-Mw) is ready to change his mind as to what constitutes immigration reform.
> 
> Surrounded by reporters after addressing the [**National Association of Latino Elected and Appointed** **Officials**](http://www.google.de/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=national%20association%20of%20latino%20elected%20and%20appointed%20officials&source=web&cd=1&ved=0CE4QFjAA&url=http://www.naleo.org/&ei=RQfqT_GlMsWl4gTdn5wa&usg=AFQjCNHO0qj7xvf4hgP92t6oJhxt_Q0AtA) in**Orlando** on June 22, **Scott**expressed a much more pragmatic approach to dealing with immigration than was heard in his initial election campaign.
> 
> “Number one, the federal government needs to have a national, secure borders. Two, have an immigration policy that everybody understands – Americans and people who come to our country. Three, we need to make sure we have a work-visa program that doesn’t put Florida businesses at a disadvantage,” said Scott.
> 
> In appealing to the conservatives of the tea party firebrand in 2010, he promised [**E-Verify** programs](http://www.dhs.gov/files/programs/gc_1185221678150.shtm) statewide to assure that suspected undocumented immigrants weren’t getting jobs in the Sunshine State.
