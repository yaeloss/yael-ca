By Marianela Toledo and Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> MIAMI— After the **Supreme Court**‘s 5-4 ruling upholding the mandatory purchase of health insurance, much concern has been raised about the now-constitutional ‘health tax’ that Americans will be required to be pay.
> 
> Florida officials were quick to point to the “ultimate deception” of President [**Barack Obama**](http://www.barackobama.com/) and the Democratic Congress that passed the bill in 2010.
> 
> “The Supreme Court said it was a tax, [which is exactly the opposite of what the president has said all along](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xETgEKmNEGM&feature=player_embedded) and what most individuals who voted for it said,” a disappointed Gov. [**Rick Scott**](http://www.google.de/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=rick%20scott%20%20&source=web&cd=9&ved=0CHUQFjAI&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.flgov.com%2F&ei=D6rtT5zGM4yL4gTLmLnMDQ&usg=AFQjCNHtbgUGu3SflgaAlHypGq12clP-Mw) said in a news conference Thursday afternoon after the ruling was announced.
> 
> “I think the right thing to do, because it was a tax, is that everyone who said that it wasn’t a tax, they need to repeal it. They need to go out and work actively to get rid of that tax.”
> 
> Scott underscored the “devastating impact” the soon-to-be-implemented health care law would have on businesses and jobs — the [same message he trumpeted while running for governor](http://watchdog.org/22326/fl-rick-scott-very-optimistic-obamacare-will-be-struck-down/) in 2010.
