By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> TAMPA — During the 2008 **Republican National Convention** in Minneapolis,  more than 10,000 supporters of Texas congressman and presidential candidate **[Ron Paul](http://ronpaul2012.com/)**gathered at a competing celebration they dubbed the “**[Rally for the Republic](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_O9T2e1OMws&feature=related)**,” complete with speeches, music and campaign flair meant to clash with the official convention across town.
> 
> The rally was organized by the Paul campaign after the congressman was [denied a speaking role](http://thecaucus.blogs.nytimes.com/2008/06/10/paul-to-host-parallel-convention/) at the main convention, mostly likely because of his refusal to endorse Republican presidential nominee and Arizona Sen. **John** **McCain**.
> 
> For the [2012 RNC in **Tampa**](http://www.gopconvention2012.com/), after a long-fought primary process which has so far accumulated close to [245 delegates](http://thereal2012delegatecount.com/) for the congressman, many of those same supporters will indeed become part of the official delegate process on the floor of the convention—but that won’t stop their plans for a new party.
> 
> Supporters of Paul are organizing a three-day long **[Paul Festival](http://www.paulfestival.org/)**, featuring political speakers, comedians, rock music and activism, according to the [website](http://www.paulfestival.org/).
