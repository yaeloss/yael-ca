By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> TAMPA — It is a standard claim that has become irrefutable mantra in **Sunshine State** politics.
> 
> “As goes the **Tampa** media market so goes **Florida**,”**Tallahassee**\-based Democratic strategist **Steve Schale**told the **[New York Times](http://fivethirtyeight.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/01/31/the-political-geography-of-florida/)** during the Republican presidential primary in January.
> 
> “As **I4** corridor goes, so goes Florida,” wrote the**[Washington Times](http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2008/jan/28/as-i-4-corridor-goes-so-goes-florida/)** editorial board at the beginning of the 2008 presidential cycle.
> 
> “As goes Florida **Hispanics**, as goes Florida,” penned the **[Tampa Bay Times](http://www.tampabay.com/blogs/the-buzz-florida-politics/content/goes-fla-hispanics-12-so-goes-fla)** in March 2011.
> 
> “As goes the I4 corridor and its swing voters, as goes Florida and maybe the whole thing,” Schale told [**Florida Watchdog**](http://watchdog.org/32013/shno-polls-show-fl-as-key-battleground-state-in-2012/) in April.
> 
> “As **Pasco (County)** goes, so does the state of Florida and so does the nation,”**former Gov. Jeb Bush** told [local Republicans](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v431U4PZEaQ) when he endorsed **Marco Rubio**for **U.S. Senate** in May 2010.
> 
> “If you win in Tampa Bay, you’ll win the state of Florida,” Tampa **Mayor Bob Buckhorn** said at **POLITICO**‘s “**[Playbook Breakfast](http://www.politico.com/events/playbook-breakfast/)**” on Tuesday in**Washington**, **D.C.**
> 
> Easily summarized, the claim made by any self-important group states that a particular region or populace will decide the outcome of an election — foregoing all other electoral shifts in other parts of Florida.
> 
> That notion is perpetuated exponentially once “bellwether” status is donned on the Sunshine State, as the close elections in 2000 proved quite easily, and the larger-than-normal electorate of swing-state voters is taken into consideration by polling experts and quantitatively minded campaign strategists.
