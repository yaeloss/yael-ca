By Yaël Ossowski | Florida Watchdog

> TAMPA— After a year and a half of the Republican presidential nomination process, it seems that the inevitable nominee will be nominated at the **Tampa Bay Times Forum** next week.
> 
> Clinging to the title of presumptive nominee, former Mass. Gov. **Mitt Romney** will take to the floor at the **Republican National Convention** to shed that name and attempt to unite the 18 million voices that cast votes in the primaries, not always in his favor.
> 
> The many months of attempting to find the “[anti-Romney](http://www.newsmax.com/AndreaTantaros/Romney-Santorum-Iowa-caucus/2012/01/05/id/423198),” the candidate without a large state government health care plan or a more liberal governing record in his past, seem to have been unsuccessful.
> 
> As [foretold](http://www.nytimes.com/2011/10/23/opinion/sunday/douthat-mitt-romney-the-inevitable-nominee.html?adxnnl=1&adxnnlx=1345838456-Zrk1DDRqAFs+KC9o6NNxSg) by **New York Times** columnist **Ross Douthat** in November 2011, the political press at that time was ready to commit to nearly a year of avoiding Romney’s ‘inevitability,’ engaging in an “extended masquerade, designed to persuade credulous readers and excitable viewers that the Republican presidential nomination is actually up for grabs.”
> 
> In March 2012,after the endorsement of future vice presidential nominee U.S. Rep. **Paul Ryan,** R-Wisc., Republican strategists and party bosses felt the same.
> 
> “This is evidence of Mitt Romney transitioning from being the frontrunner to the presumptive nominee,” senior Republican strategist **Doug Heye** told [CNN](http://www.cnn.com/2012/03/30/politics/romney-endorsements/index.html).
