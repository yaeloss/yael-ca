By Yaël Ossowski | [Vermont Watchdog](http://watchdog.org/125839/state-employees-vermont/)

In keeping with **Vermont Watchdog**‘s mission of keeping state government accountable, a quick peek at the [state payroll](http://humanresources.vermont.gov/sites/dhr/files/Documents/Workforce%20Reports/DHR-Workforce_Report.pdf) is always important.

The state of Vermont has 7,805 public employees, [according to the **Department of Human Resources**](http://humanresources.vermont.gov/), a number that has stayed relatively stable for a decade.

The average state employee in Vermont is 47 years old, gets paid $52,686 and has [worked for the government for 12 years](http://humanresources.vermont.gov/sites/dhr/files/Documents/Workforce%20Reports/DHR-Workforce_Report.pdf). There’s an 86 percent chance they’re in some kind of union.

Loaded with these facts, it’s time to unveil the 10 oddest state jobs in **Vermont** you didn’t know your taxes paid for.

## **Chief  performance officer**

[![britney](images/britney.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/britney.gif)

This high-profile position is held by Susan Zeller in the Department of Agency Administration.

## **Military storekeeper**

[![OJ](images/OJ.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/OJ.gif)

The military storekeeper works in the Department of Military and has an annual salary of $38,480.

## **Taxpayer advocate**

[![taxpayer](images/taxpayer.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/taxpayer.gif)

The state’s Taxpayer Advocate, Gloria Hobson, works in the Department of Taxes for an annual salary of $77,376.

## **State economist**

[![money](images/money.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/money.gif)

Vermont pays its state economist $69,992.

## **Director Captive Insurance**

[![insurance](images/insurance.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/insurance.gif)

The director works in the Department of Banking, Insurance, Securities for $108,243 annually.

## **Dairy products specialist**

[![milk](images/milk.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/milk.gif)

This lactose-heavy job pockets $61,277 a year.

## **Director of the commission on women**

[![equality](images/equality.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/equality.gif)

This executive position pays $76,586 a year to “help women achieve legal, economic, social, and political equality,” [according to its website](http://women.vermont.gov/about-vcw).

## **Education consultant**

[![obama](images/obama.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/obama.gif)

This high-profile job is one of the top positions in the Vermont Department of Education

## **Sex offender supervisor**

[![mistake](images/mistake.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/mistake.gif)

Housed in the Department of Corrections, this job offers $62,650 a year.

## **Fish culture specialist**

[![fish](images/fish.gif)](http://watchdog.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/2014/01/fish.gif)

Vermont has at least 15 fish culture specialists, each earning up to $75,000 a year.
